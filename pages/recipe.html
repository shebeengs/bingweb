Garlic-Butter Steak

  • Time: 20 min.
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Butter - softened, divided 2 tablespoons
Fresh Parsley - minced 1 teaspoon
Minced Garlic 1/2 teaspoon
Reduced-sodium Soy Sauce 1/4 teaspoon
Salt 1/8 teaspoon
Pepper 1/8 teaspoon
Beef flat iron steak or Boneless top sirloin steak 3/4 pounds

Step 1: Mix 1 tablespoon butter, parsley, garlic and soy sauce.

Step 2: Sprinkle steak with salt and pepper.

Step 3: In a large skillet, heat remaining butter over medium heat.

Step 4: Add steak; cook until meat reaches desired doneness (for medium-rare, a thermometer should read 135°; medium, 140°; medium-well, 145°), 4-7 minutes per side.

Step 5: Serve with garlic butter.

Spaghetti Pasta Carbonara

  • Time: 30 min.
  • Serves: 4-6
Ingredients Quantity
Extra virgin olive oil or unsalted butter 1 tablespoon
Pancetta or thick cut bacon, diced 1/2 pound
Garlic cloves, minced 1 teaspoon
Whole eggs 3-4 pcs
Grated parmesan or pecorino cheese 1 cup
spaghetti pasta (or bucatini or fettuccine) 1 pound
Salt and black pepper to taste

Step 1: Heat pasta water: Put a large pot of salted water on to boil (1 Tbsp salt for every 2 quarts of water.)

Step 2: Sauté pancetta/bacon and garlic: While the water is coming to a boil, heat the olive oil in a large sauté pan over medium heat. Add the bacon or pancetta and cook slowly until crispy.

Add the garlic (if using) and cook another minute, then turn off the heat and put the pancetta and garlic into a large bowl.

Step 3: Beat eggs and half of the cheese: In a small bowl, beat the eggs and mix in about half of the cheese.

Step 4: Cook pasta: Once the water has reached a rolling boil, add the dry pasta, and cook, uncovered, at a rolling boil.

Step 5: Toss pasta with pancetta/bacon: When the pasta is al dente (still a little firm, not mushy), use tongs to move it to the bowl with the bacon and garlic. Let it be dripping wet. Reserve some of the pasta water.

Move the pasta from the pot to the bowl quickly, as you want the pasta to be hot. It's the heat of the pasta that will heat the eggs sufficiently to create a creamy sauce.

Toss everything to combine, allowing the pasta to cool just enough so that it doesn't make the eggs curdle when you mix them in. (That's the tricky part.)

Step 6: Add the beaten egg mixture: Add the beaten eggs with cheese and toss quickly to combine once more. Add salt to taste. Add some pasta water back to the pasta to keep it from drying out.

Serve at once with the rest of the parmesan and freshly ground black pepper. If you want, sprinkle with a little fresh chopped parsley.

Garlic Parmesan Chicken Wings

  • Time: 45 min.
  • Serves: 3
Ingredients Quantity
Chicken wings 10 pieces
Garlic powder 1 tsp
Dried oregano 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Freshly grated Parmesan cheese 1/2 cup
Unsalted butter, melted 1/4 cup

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F.

Step 2: Heat up a big pot of boiling water. Season the water with salt, like you would for cooking pasta (taste the water, it should taste a little repulsively salty, like sea water).

Step 3: Boil the chicken wings for 7-8 minutes, then drain well and dry very well with a paper towel. If you don’t dry them really well now, they won’t get crispy in the oven

Step 4: Place the wings fatty side down on a sheet pan, and bake for 25-30 minutes on one side, then flip the wings and bake for another 5 minutes on the other side. The wings should have crispy crackly golden skin on the side that was in contact with the sheet pan.

Step 5: Combine the garlic powder, oregano, salt, Parmesan, and butter, then toss the chicken wings in the mixture. Serve and enjoy!

Source: Garlic Parmesan Chicken Wings
